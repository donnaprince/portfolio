Currently, I work at my local Kumon Math and Reading Center, where I am employed as a Kumon Assistant. It has been my pleasure, to have been working in the center from September 2018, till now. Some of the tasks, I do include helping students with their math and reading, by providing answers to their questions. In addition, I also correct children’s work for accuracy and neatness. Lastly, I also play the role of a team member, to many of the co-workers at the center. Over this time period, I have been able to create many bonds with the members of the center. Skills, that I have obtained from being in a paid position include: interacting with clients (children), arriving in a timely manner, and communicating with my Kumon Staff. Interacting with young children has always been a joy for me. This experience allowed me to once more, enter the mindset of young children, to help them in their academic endeavors. Arriving in a timely manner is one of the most important aspects of holding a job. Through this job, I have learned the importance of timekeeping, as one's lateness, greatly affects the dynamics of how others are able to complete their tasks. Since the center, works collaboratively, I have made it my priority to ensure, that I completed each of my shifts, with the highest quality I can provide. Upon meeting with one of my managers, she has complimented me about my ability to correct children’s classwork and excellent pace. Overall, working at Kumon has allowed to me to develop the skills needed in the work force.

As a Junior a part of the Dublin Engineering and Design Academy, I took part in the Dublin Engineering Mentorship Program. The program consists of weekly check-ins, and a once a month meeting with our mentors. In this program, I was paired up with Jayati Sengupta, Chief of Product and Marketing at CareerWaze, an AI-driven Edtech startup here in the Bay Area. Her career has spanned executive roles in both Engineering and Product Management as well as Marketing (Business) in the tech industry. She has also been a mentor for two years, mentoring Dublin Engineering and Design Academy students. The main objective of the program was to ensure that we would be prepared for the college application process, as well as develop skills that are essential to the workforce. Some of the skills we focused on during that journey were networking, how to communicate effectively through emails, developing an online portfolio, college application process, and how to set realistic goals for the future. In the mentorship process, we also faced a mock interview to prepare for the interviews we would face in the upcoming years. Another activity we participated in was career round robins, where we were able to engage with various mentors to ask about their career paths and expertise. Through the mentorship program, I was able to learn more about the various career options and skills that are needed to be a capable worker in the workforce. Aside, from this, I was also able to create a good bond with my mentor, Jayati, who has guided me in the college application process and has helped me understand what to anticipate of the workforce.
