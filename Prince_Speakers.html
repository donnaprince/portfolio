An influential speaker was Dan Cherrier, who discussed the various aspects of Civil Engineering. This speaker, outlined the importance of civil engineering, what to expect in a civil engineering job and how to get your license to practice. The speaker provided us with his background, being a manager at various companies, along with having a geotechnical engineering background. He also, explained in depth the difficulty of studying civil engineering, as it has 1 in 4 dropouts. He also explained, the five fields of civil engineering, construction, geotechnical, structural, transportation and water resources. I had never known that civil engineering had these many subsections, but this provided me with new knowledge. He also stressed the importance of getting your license to be engineering, as only licensed engineering can practice Civil, Mechanical or Electrical Engineering. He had also, revealed that this field was one that is the always in shortage, due to its challenging nature. Only through his presentation was I able to open my mind to the field of Civil Engineering. Prior to this, I also believed that this path was way to hard, and too challenging. Sharing his experience working in the field has allowed me to take into consideration, different engineering pathways besides my current track of Computer Science. Overall, his influential presentation, allowed me to learn the various tracks in Civil Engineering and the importance of getting a license for some engineering sectors.
