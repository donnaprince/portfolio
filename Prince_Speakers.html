An influential speaker was Mr.Dan Cherrier, who discussed the various aspects of Civil Engineering. This speaker, outlined the importance of civil engineering, what to expect in a civil engineering job and how to get your license to practice. The speaker provided us with his background, being a manager at various companies, along with having a geotechnical engineering background. He also, explained in depth the difficulty of studying civil engineering, as it has 1 in 4 dropouts. He also explained, the five fields of civil engineering, construction, geotechnical, structural, transportation and water resources. I had never known that civil engineering had these many subsections, but this provided me with new knowledge. He also stressed the importance of getting your license to be engineering, as only licensed engineering can practice Civil, Mechanical or Electrical Engineering. He had also, revealed that this field was one that is the always in shortage, due to its challenging nature. Only through his presentation was I able to open my mind to the field of Civil Engineering. Prior to this, I also believed that this path was way to hard, and too challenging. Sharing his experience working in the field has allowed me to take into consideration, different engineering pathways besides my current track of Computer Science. Overall, his influential presentation, allowed me to learn the various tracks in Civil Engineering and the importance of getting a license for some engineering sectors.

An influential speaker was Aileen Iniguez, who discussed the various aspects of being an analytics analyst. Iniguez mentioned how she was a newly graduated student from U.C Berkeley and took part in a program called Techbridge Girls, which has led to her job at Chevron. She mentioned how she was able to network with her mentors at the program, that introduced her to opportunities at Chevron, allowing her to obtain this position. The speaker was able to break down what a data scientist does, which is using math and science to manipulate data into an easier format. She then proceeded to outline her daily routine at Chevron, which was classified as technically oriented days and business-oriented days. On the business-oriented days, she partakes in daily stand-ups, interacts with business users and frames business questions. On the other hand, on technical days she has a daily stand up, wrangles data, codes, analyzes the data output with math and statistics, and frames analytics questions. She also mentioned how the atmosphere at Chevron, was a wonderful one as she was able to create many good experiences with her co-workers. She also mentioned that as a newly graduated student, she was given mentors at Chevron to learn more about how the company runs, and more about their specific standards. Sharing her experience working in the field has opened my eyes to the importance of networking as she was able to get to this current position, by maintaining in touch with her mentors. Overall, her presentation allowed me to learn more about data science as I have been interested in going to this pathway but did not know much about what data analysts do.
